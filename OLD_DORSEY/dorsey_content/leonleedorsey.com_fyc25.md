## Michael Wolff





PIANO



Michael Wolff, an acclaimed pianist and composer, brings a profound touch to the trio’s interpretation of Bill Evans’ legacy. Known for his intuitive playing style and rich harmonic language, Wolff’s contributions on “A Letter to Bill Evans” highlight his deep connection to Evans’ music, shaped by years of admiration and personal acquaintance.



His nuanced approach blends lyrical elegance with modern improvisational flair, making each track a fresh exploration rather than a mere tribute.



Wolff’s piano is the trio’s melodic heartbeat, infusing the album with an intimate, reflective quality that honors Evans while standing distinctly on its own

- ![A man wearing a black suit and white shirt sitting against a wooden wall, holding a decorative spherical object, with red leaves in the foreground.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/61e8380ff106b645a8ea95bd/df97ebfc-9a1d-437b-9ff3-e9f19e44097d/DORSEY_2024_sm.png)







## Leon Lee Dorsey





DOUBLE BASS



Leon Lee Dorsey stands as the rhythmic anchor of the trio, bringing a deep, resonant tone and impeccable timing to “A Letter to Bill Evans.” A seasoned bassist with a rich background in both performance and composition, Dorsey’s approach is both technically precise and emotionally engaging.



His bass lines create a fluid yet grounded foundation that allows the trio’s dynamic interplay to shine. Known for his ability to bridge traditional jazz with contemporary interpretations, Dorsey infuses each track with a sense of narrative, honoring Evans’ legacy while exploring new musical territories.



His work on this album demonstrates not only his reverence for the past but also his commitment to pushing the boundaries of modern jazz

- ![A man sitting at a piano holding drumsticks, wearing a dark blazer and black shirt, against a dark blue background.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/61e8380ff106b645a8ea95bd/b22375ba-e923-4937-9893-1f3a7d870eb0/Mike_fave_IP_edit_update.jpg)







## Mike Clark





DRUMS



Mike Clark, celebrated for his groundbreaking work with The Headhunters and his distinctively funky yet intricate drumming style, plays a crucial role in “A Letter to Bill Evans.”



Known for his dynamic approach to rhythm, Clark adds a unique layer of swing and complexity that propels the trio’s interpretations forward. His drumming is characterized by an instinctual interplay that allows the music to breathe while maintaining a vibrant pulse.



Clark’s rhythmic genius on this album reflects his decades-long experience and deep connection with jazz, making every track an engaging tribute that honors Evans’ legacy while showcasing the trio’s modern touch


## Listen Up.

For their seventh collaboration, bassist Leon Lee Dorsey and drummer Mike Clark are joined by pianist Michael Wolff for a heartfelt tribute to Bill Evans. Initially hesitant, Wolff embraced the project after the pandemic, recording two sessions that capture the spirit of Evans while allowing the trio to add their unique touch.

_A Letter to Bill Evans_ breathes new life into classics like “Gloria’s Step” and “Waltz for Debby,” blending Clark’s subtle brushwork and Wolff’s distinct piano style. This album honors Evans while showcasing the trio’s dynamic chemistry, resulting in a vibrant and swinging tribute to a jazz legend.
