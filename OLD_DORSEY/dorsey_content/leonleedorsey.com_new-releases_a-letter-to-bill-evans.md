# A Letter to Bill Evans

Sep 28

Written By [Emmett Shaughnessy](https://leonleedorsey.com/new-releases?author=61e837ad6358fb7f47db46c2)

Renowned bassist **Leon Lee Dorsey** proudly announces the release of his latest album, _Letter to Bill Evans_, under the Jazz Avenue 1 label. This exceptional recording reunites Dorsey with esteemed drummer **Mike Clark** and introduces acclaimed pianist **Michael Wolff** as they pay homage to the legendary pianist-composer **Bill Evans**.

This marks the seventh collaboration between Dorsey and Clark, whose dynamic partnership has been celebrated for its rhythmic synergy and inventive improvisations. Michael Wolff, who previously joined the duo on the 2020 release _Play Sgt. Pepper_, brings his profound connection to Bill Evans’ music to the forefront of this project.

_“Bill was immensely important to me, both musically and personally,”_ Wolff reflects. _“Initially, I was hesitant to delve into his music for this tribute. But after much thought, it felt right to honor his legacy in our own way.”_

**A Fresh Interpretation of Timeless Classics**

_Letter to Bill Evans_ captures the spirit of Evans while infusing the trio’s unique interpretations and collective musical lens. The album features thoughtful renditions of Evans’ classics:

- A bossa-flavored take on **“Gloria’s Step”**

- Swinging versions of **“My Romance,” “Peri’s Scope,” “You and the Night and the Music,”** and **“Nardis”**

- Elegant readings of **“Turn Out the Stars,” “Waltz for Debby,”** and **“Time Remembered”**


Michael Wolff’s expressive piano work shines throughout the album, from his nearly two-minute heartfelt intro on **“Time Remembered”** to his virtuosic double-octave melodies on **“You and the Night and the Music.”**

**The Trio’s Unparalleled Chemistry**

Mike Clark’s masterful drumming provides a solid foundation, showcasing his versatility and deep understanding of jazz traditions. His nuanced brushwork on the ballads complements Wolff’s piano and Dorsey’s melodic bass lines perfectly.

_“Working with Michael was a natural choice,”_ says Clark. _“He’s deeply connected to Bill’s harmonic world and brings his own voice to the music. We weren’t trying to imitate Bill Evans but to offer a heartfelt tribute through our collective experience.”_

The chemistry among the trio is palpable, stemming from decades of shared musical journeys. Wolff and Clark’s relationship dates back to the late ‘60s in the Bay Area jazz scene, where they first connected during jam sessions at San Francisco’s iconic club, the Both/And.

**A Tribute Rooted in Personal Connection**

Wolff’s personal history with Bill Evans adds profound depth to the project. As a teenager, he became obsessed with Evans’ music after hearing _Live at the Village Vanguard_. Later, as a young musician, he befriended Evans, often engaging in late-night conversations about music.

_“Bill was a mentor before I even knew what that meant,”_ Wolff shares. _“This album is a letter of gratitude and respect to someone who shaped my musical path.”_

**Experience _Letter to Bill Evans_**

Leon Lee Dorsey continues to push the boundaries of jazz while honoring its rich traditions. _Letter to Bill Evans_ is not just a tribute but a celebration of the enduring influence of one of jazz’s greatest pianists.

Immerse yourself in this captivating journey as Dorsey, Clark, and Wolff breathe new life into timeless compositions, offering masterfully fresh perspectives while still respecting the original essence of Evans’ musical offerings.
