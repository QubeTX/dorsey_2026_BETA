As a legendary bassist, composer, and arranger, **Leon Lee Dorsey**, born in 1958 in Pittsburgh, has performed with jazz luminaries that include: _Dizzy Gillespie, Wynton Marsalis, Freddie Hubbard, John Lewis, Kenny Clarke, Jon Hendricks, Gloria Lynn, Harry "Sweets" Edison, Dorothy Donegan, Stanley Turrentine, George Benson, Ellis Marsalis, Neena Freelon_ and _Terumasa Hino_.

He has also performed in big bands with the Smithsonian Jazz Masterworks Orchestra, Benny Carter, the Duke Ellington Orchestra and Charlie Persip's Superband. Other career highlights include working with Frank Sinatra at Carnegie Hall, guest appearances at the White House under Presidents Reagan and Clinton and performing with Joe Williams and the operatic diva Marilyn Horne.

He has graced jazz festivals throughout the world and recorded with Lionel Hampton, Art Blakey, Arthur Prysock, Freddie Hubbard, Dakota Staton and numerous other jazz artists.

Completing his Doctor of Music (DMA) in Double Bass Performance at Stony Brook University Graduate School (2014-2016), **Dorsey** holds Master's Degrees from the University of Wisconsin-Madison and the Manhattan School of Music, and a Double Degree in Music from Oberlin College, specializing in Classical Double Bass and Jazz Performance.

His prolific recording career includes albums such as "The Watcher" (1995), "Song of Songs" (1999), "Monk Time" (2019), "Play Sgt. Pepper" (2020), "Thank You Mr. Mabern" (2021), "Freedom Jazz Dance" (2021), "Blues on Top" (2022), and "Cantaloupe Island" (2022). His latest release is "A Letter to Bill Evans" (2024).

One of his notable contributions to the world of music is the album _"Blues on Top,"_ which features a trio formation with drummer Mike Clark and guest artist Mike LeDonne on piano. The album showcases Dorsey's and Clark's talents as they fluidly fill the musical gaps and maintain a high rhythmic energy throughout the performance.

Currently serving as an _Associate Professor of Music at Berklee College of Music_, Mr. Dorsey has also held teaching positions at Queens College (NYC), the University of Pittsburgh, and Oberlin College Conservatory of Music. He maintains that jazz requires "the highest level of knowledge, study, and training across the board," reflecting his deep commitment to both performance and education in the genre.

* * *

Beyond his impressive collaborations, **Dorsey's** artistry as a bandleader shines through his extensive discography on _Jazz Avenue 1_. Each album showcases his remarkable ability to reinvent and reimagine jazz classics while maintaining their essential spirit. This innovative approach is particularly evident in his 2020 release "Play Sgt. Pepper," where he brings fresh jazz sensibilities to timeless Beatles classics.

His 2021 album "Thank You Mr. Mabern" exemplifies his talent for creating meaningful musical tributes, while "Monk Time" (2019) demonstrates his masterful interpretation of bebop's most challenging compositions. The success of "Cantaloupe Island" (2022) further cements his reputation for creating vibrant, engaging jazz that speaks to both traditionalists and contemporary audiences.

Throughout his career, **Dorsey** has maintained a dual commitment to performance and education. As an _Associate Professor of Music at the prestigious Berklee College of Music_, he shapes the next generation of jazz musicians, sharing insights gained from his decades of experience performing with the genre's greatest names. His previous role as Coordinator of Jazz Studies at the University of Pittsburgh (2008-2011) and his position as Adjunct Professor at Queens College speak to his dedication to preserving and advancing the jazz tradition.

Most recently, "A Letter to Bill Evans" (2024) showcases **Dorsey's** continued evolution as an artist. The album represents a fresh perspective on Evans' revolutionary contributions to jazz, filtered through **Dorsey's** sophisticated musical lens. This latest release joins an impressive catalog that spans nearly three decades, beginning with his debut as a leader on "The Watcher" (1995, Landmark Records).

From concert halls to recording studios, from university classrooms to international jazz festivals, **Leon Lee Dorsey** continues to push the boundaries of jazz while honoring its rich heritage. His commitment to excellence, whether performing, recording, or teaching, has established him as one of the most versatile and respected voices in contemporary jazz.
